04 · Company formation · visual research

What Companies House actually asks, what its API will and won't let you file, and how we collapse 9 form parts into 22 screens.

Goal: incorporate a UK Ltd end-to-end inside the QPay app — name check, directors, shares, PSCs, ID verification, fee payment, certificate — without kicking the user to the Companies House website. Below is the full audit of the IN01 form (v12.0, Feb 2026), the seven Companies House APIs, the new ECCTA identity-verification regime that went live 18 Nov 2025, the fee hike that lands 1 Feb 2026, and the screen-by-screen build plan.

Pricing context

The 1 Feb 2026 fee hike doubled digital incorporation overnight.

Companies House raised fees for the first time in years, shifting to full cost recovery under the ECCT Act. For the user, formation is no longer the cheap part of starting a business. For QPay, this is both a positioning lever (absorb the fee as acquisition cost) and a pricing anchor (make the bundled £100+ cost feel like part of a higher-value product).

API landscape

Seven Companies House APIs. Only one files an incorporation today.

The critical architecture finding: the new REST JSON "API Filing" service (OAuth 2.0, clean, modern) does not yet support incorporation — only four filings are live (Transactions envelope, Registered Office Address, Insolvency, Registered Email Address). Incorporation remains exclusively on the legacy XML Gateway. No published migration date. We build on XML today, migrate when Companies House releases a REST endpoint.

Strategic decision · independent of EMI timeline

MVP ships today as a presenter. ACSP status comes via HMRC TCSP registration in parallel — 3–6 months, not 12–18. No need to wait for EMI.

Two paths, two timelines. At MVP we file IN01 as a standard XML Gateway presenter; each director / PSC brings their own personal code from GOV.UK One Login. This requires only a Presenter ID and a pre-funded Credit Account — both obtainable by email within days, zero regulatory gate. We can ship formation the moment the product is built.

ACSP is a parallel track, not a blocker. To verify identities ourselves (higher conversion, no bounce to gov.uk), we need AML supervision. The fastest path: register as a Trust & Company Service Provider with HMRC (MLRs 2017) — £300 per premises + £40 per responsible person, 3–6 months approval. This is how every UK formation agent (1st Formations, Osome, CFS, Rapid) is already AML-supervised. EMI / FCA supervision would also work but takes 12–18 months and is unnecessary for this purpose.

And we'll need ACSP anyway. From spring 2026 Companies House is making ACSP status a hard requirement for filing on behalf of third parties; from no earlier than November 2026, no ACSP = no third-party filings. Starting the HMRC TCSP application now gets us inside that window comfortably.

Sequence: Day 1 — submit HMRC TCSP application + ship formation MVP as presenter with One Login fallback. Month 4–6 — TCSP approved → ACSP registration completes within weeks → migrate user flow to in-app verification. Month 12–18 — EMI authorisation lands → business-account KYC collapses into same identity capture.

ECCTA · identity verification

Every director and PSC must already be verified before the IN01 hits the XML Gateway.

The Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023 brought identity verification into force on 18 November 2025. For new incorporations the rule is absolute: an individual director cannot be appointed, and an individual PSC cannot be named, unless their 11-character Companies House personal code is already issued and included in Parts D5 / I5 of the form. This is a blocking dependency for our flow — we must verify before we submit.

Path A · GOV.UK One Login (free, default)
User taps "Verify my identity" in QPay → deep link into One Login → provides passport / driving licence + facial check → returns to QPay with 11-char personal code. Time: 5–15 min typical. Failure rate: non-trivial for older passports, foreign passports, proof-of-address edge cases.
Path B · QPay as ACSP (mandatory by late 2026)
Same passport + liveness check, but performed inside QPay using our KYC vendor. We issue the personal code directly. User never leaves the app. Unlocked by HMRC TCSP registration + ACSP application — 3–6 months total, runs in parallel with MVP. Not dependent on EMI authorisation.
Who needs a code
(a) Every individual director being appointed on incorporation. (b) Every individual PSC with >25% shares, >25% voting rights, right to appoint/remove directors, or significant influence. (c) Subscribers who aren't directors/PSCs don't need a code for incorporation itself, but will if they later become PSCs.
Code mechanics
One-off verification per person, reusable for life across all their UK appointments. Format: XXX/XXXX/XXXX. Stored in the IN01's D5 (director) and I5 (PSC) sections. Not public — name match check at D6/I6 is also non-statutory. Existing directors & PSCs of other companies who already have a code just enter it.
Form anatomy · IN01 v12.0 (Feb 2026)

Nine parts. We ship six at MVP, defer three.

The complete IN01 covers every edge case — corporate directors, guarantee companies, bespoke articles, ORPs, trust-held PSCs. For the 95% of UK Ltds our wedge targets (private company limited by shares, 1–5 individual directors, individual shareholders, model articles) we only need Parts 1, 2 (D-block), 3, 5 (H/I), 6, 7, 8, 9. Parts 2 (C/E corporate officers), 4 (guarantee), 5 (J/K RLE/ORP) are V2+ as opt-ins. Every row below maps one form section to either a screen cluster or a deferred rail.

Payment · settlement

Companies House bills via a pre-funded Credit Account, not per-filing.

Software filing (XML Gateway) does not accept card payment per submission. Instead Companies House maintains a running Credit Account linked to our Presenter ID, pre-funded by BACS / Faster Payments. Each accepted filing deducts its fee. This is the same account that will cover post-incorp filings (confirmation statements, address changes, officer appointments, etc.) so it is a permanent piece of finance ops.

STEP 1
QPay tops up CH Credit Account
BACS / Faster Payments transfer to Companies House bank, referenced with our Presenter ID. Min practical top-up ~£200. Clears same day for Faster Payments.

STEP 2

Our XML submission includes Presenter ID + MD5-hashed auth value + memorandum attachment. We accept user's £0 checkout (free formation, QPay absorbs).

STEP 3
CH deducts £100, returns CoI
On acceptance the £100 fee is debited from Credit Account balance. Certificate of Incorporation returned within hours (same-day for submissions before 15:00).

What stays the same

Every other screen — company basics, share capital, PSCs, declarations, review, pay, submit, certificate, post-incorp actions — is byte-for-byte identical to Path A.

That means no second build. The migration from Path A to Path B is a verification-module swap: we detect the user has an ACSP-capable QPay build, hide the One Login option, show the in-app capture. Users already mid-flow on Path A finish on Path A; new flows start on Path B. Zero schema change to the IN01 XML payload — the personal code format is the same regardless of issuer.

Post-incorporation · auto actions

The filing is the start, not the end.

Formation is the entry point into the product. The moment the company number lands we trigger a series of auto-actions that would otherwise live on the user's to-do list for months. Every item below is a separate feature already planned in the Features page; this is just the orchestration unique to formation.

Business account opens
EMI KYC already completed during ID verification (same face capture, same documents). Account number, sort code, debit card order fires immediately. First Faster Payment receivable within the hour.
Documents archive
Certificate of Incorporation, Memorandum, Articles, share certificates for each subscriber, initial board minutes (first director meeting template). All PDFs. Searchable. Accountant-shareable with one toggle.
Cap table & PSC register
Populated directly from IN01 F3 (subscribers) and Part 5 (PSCs). Statutory company books auto-maintained; later share issues, transfers, dividend events all flow into the same registers.
HMRC Corporation Tax registration
Joint filing (CH + HMRC) was retired 31 March 2026. We submit CT41G directly via HMRC's API. Pre-filled from IN01 data; user reviews and confirms. Sets the Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR) within 2 weeks.
Compliance calendar seeded
Confirmation Statement due 12 months from incorporation date (now £50). First accounts due 21 months later. Corporation Tax return due 12 months after first accounting reference date. VAT quarterly if registered. PAYE monthly if registered. All entered into the Taxes tab calendar.
Accountant invite (Q channel)
If user came via Q Accountants referral, the accountant's portal auto-receives the new client file. Read-only access to books, filings, statutory registers. Q Accountants can take over any filing with a toggle.
Open decisions

Six calls before we build.

Each of these locks a significant piece of scope, cost, or regulatory exposure. They are sequenced; top of the list must be answered first.

1. Register as ACSP to verify identities in-app?
Yes, and start the AML-supervision clock immediately — but it does not block MVP. Path: apply for HMRC TCSP registration now (£300 + £40/person, 3–6 months) → ACSP follows within weeks of TCSP approval. Independent of the 12–18 month EMI track. Also becomes mandatory for third-party filings from late 2026, so the application needs to be in flight regardless.
YES · HMRC route · parallel to MVP
2. Absorb the £100 CH fee?
Competitors either don't offer formation (Tide, Revolut, Mettle, ANNA) or charge add-on pricing (Osome £289, 1stFormations £52.99 + upsells). Free formation is the strongest top-of-funnel hook possible. Breakeven: ~10 months on £15/mo plan.
YES · Year 1
3. Build on XML Gateway now or wait for REST API Filing incorporation?
CH has no published timeline for REST incorporation. Waiting means losing formation wedge indefinitely. XML Gateway is legacy but stable; plan to migrate when REST lands.
BUILD XML · migrate later
4. Support bespoke Articles of Association at MVP?
Model Articles serve ~95% of UK Ltds. Bespoke requires PDF upload flow, legal review, parsing for subsequent filings. Q Accountants partner path handles bespoke for the remaining 5%.
NO at MVP · route to Q
5. Support corporate directors / guarantee companies / ORPs at MVP?
Scope bloat vs. target customer. Corporate directors and guarantee companies sit outside the dividend / shareholder wedge entirely.
NO · V2+
6. Offer § 243 / § 790ZF residential-address protection as an add-on?
The £100 fee + 5-week wait is Companies House, not us; we simply expose the option during the director / PSC screens. Valuable for directors with credible threat models. Low build cost, high trust signal.
